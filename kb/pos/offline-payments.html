Point of Sale

Offline Payments

StallVantage can accept card payments even when your device has no internet connection. Offline charges are encrypted and stored locally, then submitted automatically when connectivity is restored.

How offline card payments work

  1. When the device goes offline during a card checkout, StallVantage detects the loss of connectivity.
  2. On your first offline card sale, an Offline Card Charges — 24-Hour Limit disclaimer is shown. Read it carefully and tap I Understand — Continue to proceed.
  3. The charge is accepted by the card reader and stored securely on the device.
  4. The Sale Complete screen shows a cloud-off icon and a reconnection deadline.
  5. Once your device is back online, pending offline charges are submitted to Square or Stripe automatically.

Important limits

Provider Max per offline transaction
Square $50,000
Stripe $10,000
  • You must reconnect within 24 hours. Charges not submitted within that window are automatically declined by the payment network — you will not receive payment.
  • The offline disclaimer is shown only once. Subsequent offline sales skip straight to checkout.

Setting a reconnection reminder

From the Sale Complete screen after an offline charge, tap Set a Reminder. StallVantage sends a push notification one hour before the 24-hour deadline so you have time to reconnect.

> Reminder notifications require push notification permission to be granted. Enable this in Settings → Notifications or in your device's notification settings.

Viewing pending offline sales

Pending offline charges appear in your Sales & Orders history with a "Pending" status badge. They update to the final status automatically after the charge is submitted.

Cash sales offline

Cash transactions are always available regardless of connectivity. No special disclaimer or reconnection requirement applies.
