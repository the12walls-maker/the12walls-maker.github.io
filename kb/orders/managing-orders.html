Managing Customer Orders

The Orders page lets you take advance orders from customers, track their status, and feed them into your prep planning automatically.

Getting there

Tap Sales & Orders on the home screen, then tap the Orders card.

Creating an order

  1. Tap New Order.
  2. Fill in the customer details:
  • Customer name — required
  • Phone number
  • Email address — used for order-ready notifications
  • Market / pickup location — type it or tap a quick-select chip from your market list
  1. Set the due date by tapping Choose, or enable Call When Ready if the customer does not need a fixed date.
  2. Set the Status (defaults to Submitted).
  3. Add at least one order item: tap Add Item, enter the product name, quantity, unit price, and an optional line note.
  4. Add any order-level notes.
  5. Tap Submit Order.

The order is saved and automatically added to your Prep List planning.

> Free plan: A maximum of 3 active orders are allowed. Upgrade to Pro or Business for unlimited orders.

Order statuses

Status Meaning
Submitted Received, not yet prepared
Ready Prepared and waiting for pickup
Completed Handed off to the customer
Canceled Order canceled

Active orders (Submitted and Ready) are shown on the Active tab. Completed and Canceled orders move to the Completed tab.

Completing an order

From the Active tab, open an order card and tap Complete. If the customer's email is on file and your email notifications are configured, StallVantage shows a preview of an order-ready email and asks whether to send it. Tap Send Email to notify the customer, or Skip to complete without sending.

Editing an order

Tap Edit on any order card to open the order editor. All fields can be changed. Tap Submit Order to save.

Exporting an order to PDF

Tap PDF on any order card to generate a printable order summary. The PDF is shared using your device's standard share sheet (save, print, AirDrop, etc.).

Tracking metrics

The top of the Orders page shows three summary cards:

  • Active Orders — orders still affecting your prep workload
  • Ready for Pickup — orders marked Ready
  • Call When Ready — customers who opted for a call instead of a fixed date
