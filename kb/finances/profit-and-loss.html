Profit & Loss Statement

The Profit & Loss (P&L) page gives you a clear picture of your revenue, cost of goods, expenses, and net profit over any date range.

> Pro / Business feature. P&L requires an active Pro or Business subscription.

Getting there

Tap Finances on the home screen, then tap Profit & Loss.

Reading the P&L

The statement is divided into sections:

Section What it shows
Revenue Total sales recorded through POS and Orders for the period
Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Ingredient costs for products sold, calculated from your recipes
Gross Profit Revenue minus COGS
Expenses All expenses you have logged in the Expenses page
Net Profit Gross Profit minus Expenses
Net Margin Net Profit as a percentage of Revenue

Filtering by date range

Use the date range picker at the top to view any period: this week, this month, a custom range, or year-to-date.

How revenue is calculated

Revenue is pulled from all completed POS sales and customer orders for the selected period. It is broken down by market/event where applicable.

How COGS is calculated

COGS is calculated from the ingredient costs in your Recipes. For each product sold, the app multiplies units sold by the cost per batch (divided by batch yield). Keeping recipe ingredient costs up to date in Inventory gives you the most accurate COGS.

Exporting the P&L

Tap Export to generate a CSV or PDF of the P&L statement. Use this with your accountant or tax preparer.
