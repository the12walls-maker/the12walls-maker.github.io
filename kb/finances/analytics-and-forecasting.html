Analytics & Forecasting

Analytics and Forecasting give you data-driven insights into your sales performance and help you plan for upcoming events.

> Pro / Business feature. Both Analytics and Forecasting require an active Pro or Business subscription.

Analytics

Getting there

Tap Finances on the home screen, then tap Analytics.

What Analytics shows

Chart Description
Revenue over time Daily or weekly revenue bar chart for the selected period
Top products Your best-selling items by revenue and units sold
Market comparison Revenue side-by-side across your different market locations
COGS trends How your cost of goods has changed over time
Gross margin trend Your profit margin over time

Filtering

Use the date range picker to analyze any period. Toggle between charts using the tabs at the top.

Market comparison

The Market Comparison chart is particularly useful for deciding which events to prioritize. It shows revenue, transaction count, and average sale value per market.

Forecasting

Getting there

Tap Finances on the home screen, then tap Forecast.

What Forecasting does

Forecasting uses your historical sales data to predict revenue and units sold for an upcoming event. Predictions take into account:

  • Historical performance at that specific market
  • Seasonal trends
  • Any manual adjustments you apply

Creating a forecast

  1. Go to Finances → Forecast.
  2. Select an upcoming event from the dropdown.
  3. Review the auto-generated forecast.
  4. Adjust any line item if you expect unusual demand (e.g., a new product, holiday market).
  5. Tap Save Forecast.

Forecast review

After the event, go to Finances → Forecast Review to compare predicted vs. actual sales. This helps improve future forecast accuracy.

Forecast settings

Go to Settings → Forecast Settings to configure:

  • How many weeks of history to include in the model
  • Whether to weight recent weeks more heavily
  • Default confidence interval
